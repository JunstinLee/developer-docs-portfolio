**Ever had one of those moments where you just want to smash your keyboard?**

One minute you're praising AI as a "productivity revolution," and the next—after just a dozen messages—it has completely forgotten your instructions, acting like a goldfish with a seven-second memory. Perhaps you are searching for **how to fix AI forgetting instructions**, or wondering **why AI generates nonsense** that looks shockingly real. Maybe you simply changed one word in your prompt, and its answer went from "perfect code" to "total garbage."

Don’t panic. The AI isn't broken, and it’s not trying to troll you. This behavior is actually determined by the "factory settings" of its brain structure.

Today, we’re skipping the complex math formulas. Let’s speak human and answer the big question: **Why does General Artificial Intelligence behave like both a genius and an idiot?** By understanding these six core concepts—from **AI tokens** to **hallucinations**—you’ll be able to tame this beast.

## Concept 1: What are Tokens in AI?

To understand how AI reads, you first need to understand **tokens vs. words**. A "Token" is the fundamental unit of data for AI. Just as we use "grams" to weigh gold or "pixels" to measure photo resolution, AI uses Tokens to measure how much information it processes. The AI doesn't see words; it only sees Tokens.

![Ripples and waves on the calm water surface](https://aivault.dev/images/ARTICLE3/ONE.webp)


To an AI, sensing Tokens is like a hypersensitive buoy floating on water—it detects even the tiniest ripple. Changing a single word is like tossing a pebble into that water; no matter how small the pebble, the ripples change pattern completely. This explains **why changing one word in a prompt changes the result** so drastically.

## Concept 2: AI Context Window Explained

If Tokens are grains of sand, then a **Large Language Model's context window** is a glass box with an opening at the top. You can put sand in, take sand out, or swap it for sand of a different color, but the box itself has a fixed capacity.

![Minimalist isometric liquid surface transitioning into sharp geometric ripples, symbolizing a central catalyst.](https://aivault.dev/images/ARTICLE3/TWO.webp)

This means that as long as you don't switch to a different AI model, the size of that glass box—its **maximum context length**—will never change, no matter how much you edit your chat history or tweak your keywords.

## Concept 3: Max Token Output (Effective Output Length)

Many users ask, "**Why does ChatGPT stop writing in the middle of a sentence?**" This is often due to Effective Output Length, which is like the capacity of the **shovel** in the AI's hand. Even if the glass box (context) is overflowing with sand, the AI can only scoop out a limited amount at one time.
![ Transparent isometric cube with vibrant sand streaming from top to bottom as a data buffer metaphor.](https://aivault.dev/images/ARTICLE3/THREE.webp)


If your task is too massive and you force it to scoop out more sand than the shovel holds, the AI starts to struggle. It might abruptly stop halfway through (**output truncation**), or just to hit the quota, it might grab old scrap material from the bottom of the box (historical memory) and shove it at you. The result? It starts rambling and completely ignores your original instructions.

In short: When you start a new chat, the AI—limited by its **generation limit**—can only output a certain amount of effective data.

Once you exceed that range, it will forget your instructions or just recycle old chat content.

## Concept 4: AI Hallucinations Meaning

**Why does AI make things up?** This phenomenon, known as **AI hallucination**, occurs because the model will **face unknown or uncertain situations by continuing to generate "reasonable" output based on existing experience.** The result might look correct, but verification proves it wrong.

![ Confident intern at an isometric desk sending an email filled with impossible geometry and abstract doodles.](https://aivault.dev/images/ARTICLE3/FOUR.webp)

It’s like a new intern at a company. You give this intern a task he doesn't fully understand. Instead of admitting it, he concocts plausible-sounding but entirely fake information, organizes it logically, and emails it to you. You open the notification thinking the job is done, only to find the content is completely erroneous.

## Concept 5: LLM Reasoning Capabilities (Intelligence)

When we talk about **AI intelligence levels**, we refer to the **upper limit** of its ability to handle complex logic and understand deep intent solely using its own "brain," without any external assistance.

![Quiet isometric scene of an intern focused on pen and paper, surrounded by floating geometric formulas and puzzles.](https://aivault.dev/images/ARTICLE3/FIVE.webp)

Imagine locking that intern in a **windowless meeting room with no Wi-Fi, no phone, just a pen and paper**. Then, you throw him a set of extremely complex Math Olympiad problems or logic puzzles. In this "closed-book" environment, the difficulty of the problems he can solve and the rigor of his logic represent his "Intelligence."

A high-intelligence AI (like a **state-of-the-art LLM**) is like a genius intern from a top university; even without the internet, he can solve most brain-burning problems through pure logical reasoning. A low-intelligence AI, however, might struggle to understand a simple tongue twister.

## Concept 6: AI Tool Calling and Agents

**AI Tool Calling** refers to the ability of an AI agent to accurately use a set of human-provided tools (like a weather API) to solve real-world problems.

Remember that intern who was faking it because he didn't know the answer? Now, imagine you equip him properly. You give him a task he doesn't know, but this time you tell him: "You can ask colleagues, you can **browse the internet**, you can look up internal files, and check past case studies."

![Minimalist isometric scene of a desk worker integrating digital and physical tasks via computer, phone, and headset.](https://aivault.dev/images/ARTICLE3/SIX.webp)

Tool Calling measures how well that intern performs the task once you’ve given him the guidance, the permissions, and the resources to find the answer.
